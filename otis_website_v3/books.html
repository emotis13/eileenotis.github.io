Walmart: Made in China

What happens when the world’s largest retailer meets the world’s largest consumer market?

Drawing on multi-store ethnography, worker interviews, labor-organizing materials, and corporate documents, Walmart: Made in China traces Walmart’s transformation within China and the transformation of work inside its stores.

The book develops the concepts of realization labor and the internal supply chain. It follows goods from receiving and stockrooms to sales floors and checkout counters, showing how multiple labor regimes are arrayed along the pathways through which commodities realize their value.

Its title carries a double meaning: Walmart’s rise rested on Chinese manufacturing, but Walmart itself was also remade by China’s labor institutions, consumer markets, regional diversity, and postsocialist legacies.

Markets and Bodies

Women, Service Work and the Making of Inequality in China

Markets and Bodies examines how China’s expanding consumer economy transformed service work into a central site for the production of classed and gendered inequality.

Based on ethnographic research in hotels, department stores, and other service workplaces, the book shows how organizations fashioned workers’ bodies, emotions, and identities for differentiated consumer markets.

Winner, Distinguished Book Award, American Sociological Association Section on Sex and Gender.
