Sociology · Political Economy · China

I study the labor through which commodities realize their value.

My research examines labor, globalization, gender, and political economy through ethnographic studies of workplaces in contemporary China.

I investigate the workers who move, stock, display, explain, and sell commodities. My recent work develops the concepts of realization labor and the internal supply chain to show how multiple labor regimes are arrayed along the pathways through which commodities realize their value.

Featured book

Walmart: Made in China

Stanford University Press, 2026

An ethnography of retail capitalism that follows commodities through Walmart’s internal supply chain and examines the distinct labor regimes that move them toward exchange.

Based on research in Walmart stores across China, the book brings together political economy, labor process theory, and the sociology of service work to examine labor at the end of the commodity chain.
